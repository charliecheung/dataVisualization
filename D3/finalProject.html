The 13th World Wushu Championship

Purpose

The purpose of this web page is to display the data taken from the 13th World Wushu Championship results and present it in a way that highlights the patterns to the user in a visually appealing way.

About

Wushu ("military art") is a Chinese martial art that focuses on internal and external energy. Wushu is separated into two categories: sanda (which is Chinese kickboxing that incorporates grappling and throws) and taolu (the choreographed component of wushu). From taolu, wushu is again separated into hand forms, short weapon, and long weapon. Athletes choreograph forms to include kicks, punches, and jumps and are judged on the degree of difficulty in which they complete these movements.

The World Wushu Championship is hosted by the International Wushu Federation (IWUF) and takes place biannually, featuring the highest competitive level within the sport. This visualization displays the name, ID, category, and score of athletes from different countries. Interact with this visualization by selecting countries, filtering data, and data between different countries with each other.

The viewer can select from any of the header links or continue scrolling to see a visualization that highlights the data. Since all the different components of the data is best highlighted a different way, I separated each of them so they can have their own visualization. Separately, the web page covers the countries, categories, scores, and athletes. Another page is dedicated to highlight key data including things like: the average score in each category, highest scores in the entire competition, medal counts, male to female ratio, highest average scores in the entire competition, and the largest and smallest categories.
